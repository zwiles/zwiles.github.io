Famous Men and Women on Wikipedia

How do the Wikipedia pages of famous men differ from those of famous women?

Background

The initial interest in this project stemed from an Atlantic article about the sexism of Wikipedia. The statistics were staggering. Less then 10 percent of Wikipedia editors are women and few of these editors are experienced, which means they have made less then 500 edits. The lack of women editors does not allow notable women for instance ,in science, to get pages made because often men do not feel the need to create pages for them. There is at least 4,400 female scientists who reach Wikipedia's standards of notability, but have not had pages created for them. Even when pages of notable women are made they are more likely to mention their gender and relationships in comparison to notable men. Their pages are also more likely to get less edits then mens

The problem is a well known one that even Wikipedia has created a wikipedia page about the gender bias. Searching the words 'sexism or gender bias on Wikipedia' comes up with countless articles about the problems of Wikipedia yet nothing has been done. There have been a few initiatives to try and change the makeup of editors on Wikipedia but they have not had glowing successes. Wikipedia is having a problem with white men writing history and leaving an important narrative that only women can bring.
